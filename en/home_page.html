    1754 - 1763

    Is The Seven Years War the first global conflict?

    We are back in 1754. Who could ever imagine one of the largest conflicts of this century be born into the darkness of the Canadian forests? Coming from England, France, Germany and even from Poland or the Netherlands, European settlers massively land in New England and New France, yet only a few goes further in the continent. The land surface surrounding the Great Lakes region is mainly uninhabited from a European perspective.

    It is the Indians who have peopled these lands.

    Far from forming a homogeneous population, the Indians who have lived there speak diverse languages, practice miscellaneous rituals and are shaping powerful networks. Besides, they are developing trade with European trappers and other adventurous settlers. Commercial and diplomatic exchanges are gaining momentum, still tensions are persisting. They are notably fueled by the current French-English enmity, but also by the successive spoliations of Indians lands, which are becoming an object of speculation.

    At the Pennsylvania’s margins, since 1747, the British colonial administration has undertaken and planned the colonization of the Ohio river valley. Ohio means “beautiful river” in Seneca, an Iroquoian language. At that time, it created the Ohio company and has built many fortresses to protect the area; it has organized the land speculation on these fertile plains with a relatively soft climate compared to those of Quebec and New England. Dozens of families have come to settle in this valley since. Yet these lands are also claimed by New France, which have built some forts around in retaliation. The first skirmishes will not delay in provoking a deepening animosity between the two powers, which will spread later in Europe and around the globe.

    Thus, At the origins of the Seven Years War, also known as the French and Indian War in America, we find small clashes in North America. These fights, even if they are not the only responsible, will have disastrous effects on the fragile European geopolitical balance, which stems from the War of the Austrian Succession. Therefore, the historical study of the beginning of the Seven Years War has two sides. It is on one hand the analysis of this sinking into the abyss of the war and on the other hand the review of this spread; owing to the fact that in the long march of globalization, this conflict gets all the features of a global conflict, or at least a globalized conflict. This web site endeavours to highlight those characteristics in the first time.

    In the second time, this web site is committed to exhibit the difference and the evolution of the historiographical points of view on this conflict, as our common perceptions of the Seven Years War have continuously evolved through two hundred and fifty years of political and geopolitical history, and diverge following which belligerents bear witness to this era.
